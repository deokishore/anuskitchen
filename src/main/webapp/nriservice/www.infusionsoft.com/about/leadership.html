Leadership at Infusionsoft

At Infusionsoft, we are all leaders. We expect all Infusionites to co-create and execute plans that deliver results, inspire commitment to our vision, and accelerate the growth of those around them.

Learn more about the Leadership Model

Executive Management

Infusionsoft is managed at the highest levels by innovators, industry experts, tech geeks, and small business zealots. They are the executives that help mold us into the leaders we want to become. They’re forging ahead on our mission to help small businesses succeed, and setting a clear vision for the future of Infusionsoft.

Clate Mask

Chief executive officer and Co-founder
Connect:
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn

Clate has been educating and inspiring entrepreneurs for over a decade, and is recognized by the small business community as a visionary leader. His passion for small business success stems from his personal experience taking Infusionsoft from a struggling startup to an eight-time Inc. 500/5000 winner. As CEO, Clate is leading Infusionsoft on its mission to create and dominate the market of sales and marketing software for small businesses.

Under Clate's leadership, Infusionsoft has landed four rounds of venture capital including a $55 million Series D led by Bain Capital Ventures with contributions from prior investors, Signal Peak Ventures and Goldman Sachs. He was named an Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year finalist, a Top 100 Small Business Influencer by Small Business Trends, one of the 100 Most Intriguing Entrepreneurs of 2013 by Goldman Sachs, and Business Leader of the Year by the Arizona Technology Council.

Clate is a national speaker on entrepreneurship and small business success, and co-author of the New York Times bestseller Conquer the Chaos: How to Grow a Successful Small Business Without Going Crazy. Clate has a B.A. in Economics from Arizona State University, as well as an MBA and a J.D. from Brigham Young University.

Scott Martineau

Co-founder and senior vice president of product strategy
Connect:
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn

Since founding Infusionsoft in 2004 with his brother Eric, Scott has been on a mission to revolutionize the way small businesses grow. As co-founder of Infusionsoft and senior vice president of product strategy, Scott leverages his entrepreneurial background, keen understanding of small business, and expertise in Lifecycle Marketing to help drive Infusionsoft forward. He is the co-author of the New York Times bestseller Conquer the Chaos: How to Grow a Successful Small Business Without Going Crazy.

Scott has been instrumental in fostering an award-winning culture at Infusionsoft, which has been named a “Best Place to Work” for seven years running. He holds a B.S. in computer information systems from Arizona State University.

Curtis Smith

Chief financial officer
Connect:
  • LinkedIn

Curtis is a proven Silicon Valley finance executive with a unique blend of public SaaS and technology investment banking experience. As Infusionsoft's chief financial officer, he oversees the company’s corporate finance, accounting, legal and investor relations departments.

Prior to joining Infusionsoft, Curtis spent more than three years as CFO of Keynote Systems, a public SaaS company specializing in cloud testing and monitoring, where he drove revenue growth, profitability expansion and operational efficiencies. Additionally, he has advised leading technology companies and executed multiple IPO and M&A transactions as a Managing Director at GCA Savvian Advisors and as a Vice President at Credit Suisse First Boston’s Technology Group.

Curtis received his undergraduate degree at Brigham Young University and later went on to earn his MBA at University of Southern California's Marshall School of Business.

Terry Hicks

Chief product officer
Connect:
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn

Terry joins Infusionsoft after 15 years in leadership roles at Intuit. He understands how the right software solutions, services and access to a strong partner network can positively impact the lives of small business owners. He led the creation and growth of numerous businesses and QuickBooks ecosystem offerings including QuickBooks Simple Start and QuickBooks Online Payroll, and established from the ground up the now $600M payments business. In his most recent role, Terry was vice president and general manager of Intuit QuickBooks Online, where he grew the business from 450,000 US subscribers to more than 840,000 subscribers in over 120 countries.

As Infusionsoft's chief product officer, Terry drives the company’s global product strategy, including product management, payments and business development. He also leads Infusionsoft's Marketplace, which is comprised of a full suite of services, integration APIs, and apps designed to help small businesses succeed. He’s passionate about small business success and works to simplify the lives of entrepreneurs around the world.

Outside of work, Terry enjoys spending time with family and friends; exploring nature on foot, bicycle or skis; advising entrepreneurs on how to solve complex problems with simple experiences; and mentoring people to see possibilities beyond their current realities and achieve success they otherwise thought unimaginable.

Hal Halladay

Chief people officer
Connect:
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn

Hal is a seasoned SaaS executive with over two decades of experience in leadership, corporate development and culture. He preserves and fosters Infusionsoft’s award-winning workplace culture while increasing operational excellence through people, process and systems.

Hal has a strong entrepreneurial background: He co-founded NorthSky, a website hosting company that was acquired by About.com, as well as a blog network that served small business. He has seven years of investment banking experience and led Infusionsoft’s acquisitions of CustomerHub and GroSocial.

Hal received an MBA from the University of Chicago Booth School of Business and B.A. in finance from the University of Utah.

Elizabeth Pitt

Chief customer officer
Connect:
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn

Elizabeth leads Infusionsoft's consulting, support and customer satisfaction teams to deliver a delightful end-to-end experience for customers. Her background in product management and customer-driven innovation, as well as her experience as an entrepreneur and Infusionsoft customer, gives her a unique insight into what small businesses need to succeed.

Prior to joining Infusionsoft, Elizabeth founded Growth Choice Enterprises, a growth consulting, coaching and training company. She oversaw new business initiatives at Intuit, where she served as director of product management, and spent several years as a senior associate at McKinsey & Company. Elizabeth has an M.B.A. in entrepreneurship, marketing and strategy from University of Chicago Booth School of Business and a B.A. in liberal arts from University of California, San Diego.

Aaron Stead

Chief sales and marketing officer
Connect:
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn

As chief sales and marketing officer, Aaron is instrumental in driving Infusionsoft's growth strategy through direct sales, marketing, partner programs and business development. A proven leader with extensive background in leading mid-market CRM software sales, Aaron has a strong track record of success in scaling people and departments to drive revenue.

Prior to joining Infusionsoft, Aaron grew several North American revenue channels for UK-based Sage, a global CRM software firm. He has an MBA from the Ken Blanchard College of Business at Grand Canyon University and a B.A. in Business Administration and Communication from Arizona State University.

Joseph DiSabato

Managing director,
Merchant Banking Division,
Goldman Sachs Group

Joe DiSabato has been the managing director in the Principal Investment Area at Goldman Sachs Group, Merchant Banking Division since 2000. DiSabato joined Goldman Sachs in 1988 and served as a Financial Analyst in Corporate Finance in New York until being named managing director in 2000 and partner in 2004.

DiSabato serves on the boards of multiple technology companies including American Traffic Solutions, Endurance International Group, Inc., and Backoffice Associates, LLC.

DiSabato holds an MBA from the Anderson Graduate School of Management at the University of California at Los Angeles and a B.S. from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

Deepak Sindwani

Partner,
 Bain Capital Ventures

Deepak Sindwani, partner at Bain Capital Ventures, focuses on growth-stage investment opportunities in leading sales and marketing software companies.

Prior to joining Bain Capital Ventures in 2009, Sindwani was a senior investment professional with Comcast Ventures, where he led several early-stage and growth-stage investments in the media and communications sectors. He was also a co-founder of an Internet software startup and an investment banker with Credit Suisse’s Technology Group.

Sindwani received a BS in Economics from the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania and a B.S. in electrical engineering from the School of Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania.

Geoffrey Moore

Venture partner,
 Mohr Davidow Ventures

Geoffrey has made the understanding and effective use of disruptive technologies the core of his life’s work. He is widely recognized for his expertise in market development and business and investment strategies and has consulted with some of the leading companies in the technology industry.

Geoffrey is best known as a best-selling author of books like Crossing the Chasm, Inside the Tornado, The Gorilla Game and Dealing with Darwin, which are often required reading in business schools across the country. Prior to his work as a consultant and writer, Geoffrey was a principal and partner at Regis McKenna, Inc., a leading high-tech marketing strategy and communications company.

Nancy Schoendorf

Partner emeritus,
 MDV

A general partner at Mohr Davidow Ventures since 1993, Ms. Schoendorf has 39 years of industry experience in a variety of engineering and executive positions and in venture capital. Her investment focus has been in the areas of software and services.

Prior to joining MDV, Schoendorf spent 10 years in engineering and engineering management positions at Hewlett-Packard. She later joined Software Publishing Corp. (SPCO), creator of Harvard Graphics, serving as an officer and vice president of R&D and product development. Following SPC, she moved to Sun Microsystems where she managed the development of the Solaris Kernel and operating system.

Schoendorf sits on the board of Shutterfly (SFLY) and several private companies. Her investments include Actuate (BIRT), Agile Software (AGIL: acquired by Oracle), Brickstream, Broadbase (acquired by Kana), Datasweep, (acquired by Rockwell Automation) Freshwater Software (acquired by Mercury Interactive), Onvia (ONVI), Infusion Software, and Panasas.

Schoendorf earned a BA with distinction in computer science and mathematics from Iowa State University and an MBA with honors from the University of Santa Clara.

Scott Petty

Managing director,
 Signal Peak Ventures

Scott co-founded Signal Peak Ventures (FKA vSpring Capital) in 2000 and has been a Managing Director since its formation. Earlier in his career as a consultant with Bain & Company, Scott worked with multiple IT and non-IT companies, re-engineering their strategies for optimum results. In addition Scott previously was COO and a Board Director of Zuka Juice, a retail organization selling nutritional products. Under Scott’s leadership, Zuka Juice grew from its first store to 96 retail units and over 400 employees before being acquired by Jamba Juice (NASDAQ:JMBA), a venture backed competitor.

Scott is a past member of the Board of Directors for the Rocky Mountain Venture Capital Association. He also has served on the Board of Directors of the Lassonde New Venture Development Center at the University of Utah. In addition Scott is a past member of the National Directors Council at the Utah Museum of Fine Arts.

Scott received a B.S. in economics from Brigham Young University and an MBA from the Harvard Business School. Some of the investments Scott has led at Signal Peak Ventures include comScore (NASDAQ:SCOR), Infusionsoft, Booker, Alianza, Control4 (NASDAQ:CTRL), Experticity, Cerberian (acquired by Blue Coat Systems), Chatbooks, MediConnect (acquired by Verisk Analytics), Public Engines (acquired by Motorola), Radiate Media, and FamilyLink (acquired by MyHeritage).

Scott grew up in Palo Alto but likes the skiing better in Salt Lake City. He enjoys road biking the Wasatch canyons and traveling with his wife and three children.

Eric Keller

Audit chair,
Infusionsoft

Eric Keller served as the chief operating officer of Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers, a venture capital firm, from 2008 to 2014. Previously he was CEO of Movaris, a private software company providing a suite of financial governance applications.

Before Movaris, Keller serviced as chief financial officer for application service provider Corio, business communications company Aspect Communications, medical device manufacturer Ventritex and scientific instrument firm Dionex.

In 2006, Keller was named one of the "Most 100 Influential People in Finance" by Treasure & Risk Magazine.

He holds a B.S. degree from Cornell University and an MBA degree from the University of California, Berkeley.
